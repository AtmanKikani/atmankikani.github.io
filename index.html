Research Spotlight

HADVel: Hydrodynamics Aware Deep Velocity Estimation

From hardware sensors (DPSS) to physics-informed deep learning for real-time AUV velocity estimation

Problem: Traditional velocity estimation on AUVs suffers from sensor noise and latency. DPSS provided hardware-level fusion; HADVel elevates this with physics-informed neural networks.

Solution: A hybrid LSTM architecture that combines hydrodynamic constraints with multi-sensor fusion, achieving 50 Hz real-time performance and sub-cm/s accuracy—42% noise reduction over baseline filters.

MARVO: Marine-Adaptive Radiance-aware Visual Odometry

Transformers meet underwater SLAM for robust visual-inertial odometry in turbid environments

Challenge: Underwater SLAM fails in turbid, low-visibility conditions due to color attenuation and light scattering.

Approach: MARVO combines Transformer-based feature matching with physics-aware radiance correction and RL-based pose graph optimization, enabling robust autonomous navigation in challenging marine environments.

Key Innovation: Physics-aware radiance correction models color-channel attenuation with depth
Architecture: Multi-sensor fusion (visual + IMU + barometer) via GTSAM + RL-based optimization
Venue: Submitted to CVPR 2026
